Welcome to MIT AI Venture Studio 


Overview

Join our AI for Impact: Venture Studio class. This course focuses on opportunities in the broad sectors of digital health, climate change, and mobility, with data- and AI-centric technologies such as blockchain, Web3, and privacy tech, which can accelerate major positive impact worldwide. Mentors consisting of CEOs, VCs, and academics work with student teams throughout the course. Students develop business plans suitable for real-world impact, which are eligible for seed funding dependent on judges' scores from demo day during the final class.

Thank you for your understanding and cooperation. ============ Confidentiality Notice ====== We are bringing to your attention issues regarding confidentiality and IP of your work. This has not been an issue in the past and we expect all class work to be not confidential. Class work of students is shared with each other to keep the highly collaborative atmosphere. The demo day is similar to MIT $100K or YCombinator demo day where all presentations are recorded and posted online. For Class Presentations and Demo Day, it is essential to understand that intellectual property protection is the responsibility of each team presenting. Teams should refrain from revealing any information that could expose unprotected IP. It is crucial to operate under the assumption that all work showcased may become publicly visible, and no confidentiality should be assumed. To safeguard sensitive information, teams should exercise caution in their presentations, avoid disclosing proprietary details that are not adequately protected, and focus on highlighting their projects value proposition, traction, and potential for growth without compromising critical IP assets. By being mindful of what is shared and ensuring that only non-sensitive information is presented, teams can navigate Class Presentations and Demo Day effectively while protecting their intellectual property. Your work in shared documents is shared only with other class participants and mentors. We also discourage visitors from taking photos of your class presentations. However, there is always a chance of a leak and we cannot guarantee that the information will not become available to those outside the class at some point. Hence we strongly encourage you to not share anything confidential during class work or on demo day. Contact us if you have any questions. =======
