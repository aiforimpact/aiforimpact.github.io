AI for Impact: Venture Studio (MAS.664)

Spring'22



Overview

Join our AI for Impact: Venture Studio (MAS644 Spring'22) class. This course will focus on opportunities in the broad sectors of climate change, health, mobility and behavior change with data and AI centric technologies such as Web3, privacy-tech, geospatial analysis and blockchain which can enable or accelerate major positive impact throughout the world. This semester’s course will be structured as a “Venture Studio” with mentors and engagement with VCs, CEOs and Academics.

Given the overwhelming interest and our limited capacity (due to room size and COVID restrictions) this course will have a two-step selection process: (1) Questionnaire, and (2) finishing the Online AI blockchain course. Please respond to the questionnaire. If you are selected after Step 1, you will be expected to finish Step 2 and upload the snapshot that you’ve finished the course. Stage 1: Questionnaire (Link 1) and Stage 2: Finish online AI/Blockchain course (Link 2) (If you have finished a degree course or have a professional background in these areas, you do not need to finish the online course). Please join the Facebook group (https://www.facebook.com/groups/1269120520232017/) and post your questions there. Also, please sign up for future updates and share them with your colleagues and on your mailing lists.

Course Outline

Class Goals:

  • "Visualize Venture Studio" format for-credit course where instructors seed and stimulate high-impact, high-value projects that are attractive for the best engineering, design, and builder students
  • Advance "Big Ideas" into MVP-seed-stage commercial consideration

Themes and Technologies:

  • Themes can include, but are not limited to: Climate Change, Mobility, Health, and Behavior Change
  • Technologies can include, but are not limited to: Blockchain, Big Data / AI, Computer Vision, Geospatial, and Persuasive Technology

Class Structure:

  • Studio to focus on 4 projects with 5-8 students per team
  • Instructors will organize guest speakers to discuss technologies and applications for global impact
  • Student teams will develop a minimum viable product (MVP) by the end of the term to be critiqued by outside invited reviewers
  • Instructors will organize a "demo day" and invite Boston, Miami and Bay Area community to engage students

Timeline:

  • Meet and Greet held at MIT/HARVARD in December
  • IAP get together (Week of Jan 10th or 17th)
  • First day of class is Feb 3rd
  • Mid-term review (Week of March 13th before Spring Break)
  • Final Class Presentations and “demo day” awards (Week of May 16th)

Support for Students:

  • Weekly class mentor meetings on Wednesday PM / guest lectures / work sessions
  • Class visits to local start-ups and technology companies
  • Data sets
  • Data and AI scientists from startups for experience
  • Sessions with startup-coaches

Funding:

  • Teams will receive milestone based seed funds

Expected Mentors and Speakers:

  • Profs / Lecturers: Jinhua Zhao, Daniela Rus, Cynthia Breazeal, Hari Balakrishnan, Fadel Adib
  • CEOs: David Morczinek (AirWorks), Kartik Venkataraman (Akasha Imaging), Shahid Azim (REACT Neuro), Rajeev Surati (Skyline Nav AI Inc), Kuldeep Singh Rajput (Biofourmis), Aditya Khosla (PathAI), Dan Raviv (Lendbuzz)
  • VCs: John Hawthorne (2Lantern), Eric Chen (OVO Fund), Calvin Chin (E14 Fund), Habib Haddad (E14 Fund), Dave Blundin (Link Ventures), John Werner (Link Ventures), Sven Strohband (Khosla Ventures), Bilal Zuberi (Lux Capital), Jon McNeil (DVx Ventures), Katie Rae (The Engine), Paul English (Boston Venture Studio), Vinod Khosla (Khosla Ventures), Jason Pontin (DCVC), Steve Jurvetson (Future Ventures), Reed Sturtevant (The Engine)
