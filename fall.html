AI and Web3 for Impact: Venture Studio (MAS.665)

Fall'22



Overview

Join our AI and Web3 for Impact: Venture Studio class. This course will focus on opportunities in the broad sectors of digital health, sustainability and mobility with data and AI centric technologies such as blockchain, Web3, and privacy-tech, which can enable or accelerate major positive impact throughout the world. This semester’s course will be structured as a “Venture Studio” with mentors and engagement with VCs, CEOs and Academics. Given the overwhelming interest and our limited capacity (due to room size and MIT regulations) this course will have a two-step selection process:


  • Step 1: Questionnaire (Link 1)
  • Step 2: Finishing the Online AI blockchain course (Link 2)


If you are selected after Step 1, you will be expected to finish Step 2 and upload the snapshot that you’ve finished the course. If you have finished a degree course or have a professional background in these areas, you do not need to finish the online course.

Course Outline

Class Goals:

  • "Visualize Venture Studio" format for-credit course where instructors seed and stimulate high-impact, high-value projects that are attractive for the best engineering, design, and builder students
  • Advance "Big Ideas" into MVP-seed-stage commercial consideration

Themes and Technologies:

  • Themes can include, but are not limited to:
    • Digital health
    • Sustainability
    • Mobility
    • Behavior change
  • Technologies can include, but are not limited to the following technologies that are essential to the 21st century:
    • Blockchain
    • Big data
    • AI
    • Computer vision
    • Geospatial

Timeline:

  • Meet and Greet held at MIT/Harvard in August
  • First day of class: September 9, 2022
  • Mid-term review: October 14, 2022
  • Final Class Presentations and “demo day” awards: December 9, 2022

Class Structure:

  • Venture studio format, with team projects with 5-8 students per team
  • Guest speakers share leading-edge thinking
  • Minimum Viable Product (MVP) by the end of the term
  • Demo Day

Support for Students:

  • Weekly class mentor meetings on Friday after class / guest lectures / work sessions
  • Class visits to local start-ups and technology companies
  • Data and AI scientists from startups for experience
  • Sessions with startup coaches

Funding:

  • Teams will receive milestone-based seed funds
