AI Studio Course

Products and Infrastructure for AI Agents

MAS.665

MAS.664 / MAS.665 / EC.731 / IDS.865

(prev: Foundations of AI Ventures, AI Venture Studio, AI for Impact)



Overview

Build products and infrastructure for AI agents as the primary users: APIs, tools, services, protocols, environments, and trust layers that autonomous agents can discover, call, compose, and rely on.

Build for AI Agents as Users

Students learn how to design systems that agents can use directly, with people acting as supervisors, beneficiaries, or operators rather than the primary users.

  • Create agent-consumable APIs, tools, services, and workflow surfaces.
  • Build infrastructure for identity, memory, permissions, payments, observability, evaluations, and trust.
  • Validate whether agents can discover, understand, call, and compose what you build.

Course Pillars

  • Innovation (20%): understand where AI is going in 2-3 years, including autonomous workflows, agent protocols, multimodal reasoning, robotics, and agent economies.
  • Agent Products and Infrastructure (30%): build products where AI agents are the primary users and people set goals, constraints, and accountability.
  • Technical Building (50%): create agent-consumable systems, LLM-based tools, service endpoints, and automated pipelines using current AI stacks and frameworks.

Course Format

  • Weekly class meetings with technical lectures, discussion, and applied examples.
  • Regular assignments and quizzes to build fluency with agent tools, APIs, evaluation, and deployment.
  • Hands-on project work throughout the semester, with workshops when scheduled.
  • A team final project with checkpoints, mentor feedback, and a technical Demo Day in December 2026.

Final Project Deliverable

  • One working agent-facing product, service, protocol, or infrastructure component.
  • The final artifact should be usable by AI agents through a clear interface, API, tool contract, or runtime environment.
  • Teams must document how agents use it and show evidence that it works under realistic agent use.
  • The semester ends with a concise final presentation and live technical demo.

Projects and Demo Day

Fall 2026 projects will center on agent-facing products and infrastructure: things AI agents can use as clients, collaborators, buyers, delegates, or operators in an agentic web environment.

Build Theme

  • Students may build agent-facing products, API-backed services, tool surfaces, infrastructure modules, agent environments, or security and reliability systems.
  • Projects should make clear what an AI agent can do with the product or infrastructure, and what contract the system exposes to agents.
  • Projects should be designed for environments where multiple agents, services, and infrastructure components interact.
  • Examples include identity, reputation, permissions, payments-style flows, memory, observability, evaluations, red-team agents, service marketplaces, and workflow automation.

Demo Day Rules

  • Demo Day is planned for December 2026; exact date TBA.
  • Final demo title, team roster, URL, deck, and table or booth needs are due two weeks before Demo Day.
  • After the freeze date, no new projects, last-minute demo swaps, table changes, or major logistics changes are accepted.
  • Expected demo length is 2-3 minutes per project, with timing enforced.
  • Teams that miss the freeze date or do not present may receive no or reduced project/demo credit.

Mentor Feedback

  • Each project is expected to meet with at least one technical mentor.
  • The target is three mentor touchpoints during the semester.
  • Mentors will be matched to a small number of projects so feedback can be specific.
  • Teams should come to mentor sessions with specific technical questions, a working artifact, and clear next steps.

Technical Support

  • Technical workshops and support sessions may be offered during the semester.
  • Topics and timing will be announced after the final course schedule is set.
  • Peer groups may be formed for homework review and project feedback.

Grading

Final grading follows the course pillars: Innovation (20%), Agent Products and Infrastructure (30%), and Technical Building (50%). Attendance, quizzes, peer feedback, mentor engagement, and Demo Day readiness are evaluated through those pillars.

Grade What it means
A+ Exceptional technical build, original insight, clear validation that agents can use the product or infrastructure reliably, polished live demo ready before the freeze date, clear documentation, active peer feedback, and three strong mentor touchpoints.
A All required work completed on time, strong working MVP or infrastructure component, clear agent-user contract, good technical execution, active participation, and on-time Demo Day presentation.
A- Complete and serious work with minor gaps in technical depth, validation, polish, documentation, or consistency. Demo works, but the project is less robust or less ambitious than A-level work.
B+ / B Substantial participation and a partial working project, but one major area is weak: missed milestone, thin technical implementation, limited validation, weak collaboration, or incomplete final demo readiness.
B- / C Inconsistent participation, late or missing assignments, limited project progress, poor communication, or a demo that does not show a credible working system.
No credit / Fail Failure to submit required work, failure to present when required, academic integrity problems, or repeated lack of engagement with course logistics and staff.

FAQ

Can non-technical students take the course?

Yes, if they are ready to build. Teams should balance product, design, research, and engineering strengths.

Are course homeworks part of admission?

No. The required admission mini-build is part of application review. Regular course homework starts after admission and happens during the course.

Is collaboration allowed?

Yes. Peer groups and mentor feedback are part of the course. Students should help each other learn while submitting their own work and documenting team contributions clearly.

Who should mentor?

Technical founders, engineers, researchers, and industry partners who can help students build and evaluate real agentic systems should contact the course team.
